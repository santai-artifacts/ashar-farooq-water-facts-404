Why It Matters

Water sustains everything we know

From the cells in your body to the crops in your fields, water is the universal solvent that makes life possible.

🧬

Biological Foundation

Every chemical reaction in your body depends on water as a solvent, transport medium, and temperature regulator.

🌾

Agriculture & Food

70% of global freshwater use goes to agriculture. Growing 1 kg of wheat requires roughly 1,500 litres of water.

⚡

Energy Production

Hydropower provides about 16% of the world's electricity, making water one of our most powerful renewable resources.

🌍

Climate Regulation

Oceans absorb over 90% of the excess heat from climate change and drive global weather patterns and currents.

Fascinating Facts

Numbers that put water in perspective

97%

of Earth's water is saltwater in the oceans — too salty to drink or use for irrigation without treatment.

4B yrs

Water has existed on Earth for roughly 4 billion years. The water in your glass may have once passed through a dinosaur.

~8 min

A person can survive roughly 3 days without water. Some desert-adapted organisms can pause life itself until water returns.

326M

Cubic miles of water exist on Earth in all forms — oceans, glaciers, groundwater, rivers, lakes, and the atmosphere.

0°C

Water is the only natural substance found in all three states — solid, liquid, and gas — at temperatures common on Earth's surface.

10,000+

Distinct bodies of water have been named on Earth, from the Pacific Ocean to hidden underground aquifers below deserts.

Take Action

Small changes, massive impact

Freshwater is finite. Here's how you can help protect it every single day.

🚿

Shorten Your Shower

Cutting your shower by just 2 minutes saves up to 10 gallons of water — add up to real change over a year.

🌧️

Collect Rainwater

A simple rain barrel can capture hundreds of gallons to water your garden without touching the tap.

🔧

Fix Leaks Fast

A dripping faucet wastes over 3,000 gallons per year. A running toilet can waste 200 gallons in a single day.

🥦

Mind Your Diet

Eating less meat significantly reduces your water footprint — beef requires ~1,800 gallons per pound produced.

🌱

Plant Native Species

Native plants are adapted to local rainfall patterns and require far less irrigation than exotic ornamentals.

🧹

Skip the Hose

Use a broom instead of a hose to clean driveways and sidewalks — saves up to 80 gallons each time.
